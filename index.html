03 Grinders — the real boss fight

The Dedica needs a fine, consistent, espresso-capable grind. Filter grinders and "esp-capable" liars will give you sour, gushing shots. Filter by budget & type.

⚡ Get an electric grinder if you possibly can. A HiBrew G5 or Rift 64 will save your mornings — almost everyone who started with a hand grinder upgraded within months. As the group put it: "go electric… unless you're stubborn and prefer arm workouts." 💪 Manual grinders (K6 / C3 ESP) make great espresso too — they're just a daily wrist day.

    06 Dial-in: the actual recipe

    Stop trusting the buttons (the "2x" button pulls a lungo). Get a scale, weigh everything, change one variable at a time.

    ▶ 🏆 The best dialing-in tutorial, period. If you watch one video before touching your machine, make it this one. The group's most-recommended guide to actually dialing in your espresso. — youtube.com ↗

    🎯 The starting point

    Dose inShot outTimeRatio
    Double18 g36 g25–32 s1:2
    Single9 g~22 g25–30 s1:2.5
    Light roast18 g45–54 glonger1:2.5–1:3

    Timer starts the moment you press the button (coffee-water contact), not at first drip.

    1. Weigh your dose. ~17–18g fills a stock double basket nicely (with a puck screen).
    2. RDT: one spritz of water on the beans to kill static.
    3. Grind straight into the basket.
    4. WDT: stir with a thin needle to break clumps & distribute.
    5. Level & tamp until it stops moving. (You basically can't tamp "too hard" — you'd need a hydraulic press.)
    6. Puck screen on top, lock in, and pull onto a scale.
    7. Taste & adjust → see "Fix My Shot" below. 👇
    🌡️ Run a hotter shot (the temperature-surfing trick). The Dedica's little thermoblock cools down between heating and brewing, which is exactly why light roasts come out sour. The fix the group swears by (via Tom's Coffee Corner): right before you pull, run a short blank flush of hot water through the empty group head to pre-heat it, then lock in and brew immediately — your shot runs noticeably hotter and extracts sweeter. Watch the trick ↗ (and a second take ↗).
    🍖 Pull a "salami shot" to train your palate. Put 3–4 small cups under a (bottomless) shot and swap them every few seconds as it pours. Taste each in order: the first pours sharp & sour (the acids come out first), the middle is sweet & balanced, and the last turns thin & bitter. Doing this once teaches you what "sour vs bitter" actually tastes like — so the "Fix My Shot" advice below finally clicks. ☕

    08 🥛 Steaming milk on a weak little wand

    The Dedica's wand is… polite. Technique > power. Here's the group consensus.

    1. Purge first. Run the wand 20–30s until you get real steam pressure. 5 seconds is not enough — "an un-purged wand is useless."
    2. Cold milk, right jug. Chill the milk (and the pitcher in the freezer). Lower-fat tetra milk foams easier for beginners — Amul Blue or Akshayakalpa are group favourites.
    3. Stretch: tip just at the surface → listen for a soft "tsss… tsss" as you inject air for ~3–5s.
    4. Texture: submerge slightly to create a whirlpool and heat through, no more hissing.
    5. Stop at ~60–65°C. Above 65°C milk proteins break and it'll never be silky again (and it scalds).
    ☕ Cappuccino = more foam · Latte / flat white = less, silkier.
    ⚖️ ~140g milk ≈ just below the Dedica mug spout. Use ~100g for a hotter, less-milky drink.
    🧼 Purge & wipe the wand immediately after every steam or it clogs.

    • Amazon gift-card trick: buy a gift card at ~11% off, pay with it + a cashback credit card (e.g. HDFC Millennia / Amazon Pay) to stack discounts on machines & beans.

    • Open-box is the smart-money buy. ~₹12k (10k+ on a good day) vs ~₹20k new on Amazon, almost always basically new, and it keeps the full 1-year warranty. A couple of buyers hit a flaw (cracked tank / dent / one pump fail) — all sorted under warranty. Film one unboxing video and the risk is tiny. (More in the FAQ. 👇)
    • Buy beans by roast date, not brand hype. Store pickup (Blue Tokai) = freshest.
